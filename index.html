Several rulings in the district court case United States v. Handley explicitly state that child pornography produced with zero victims is not obscene under both the Ferber and Miller tests, and cannot be constitutionally outlawed.
Obscenity laws, in addition to not typically being used for people enjoying things privately (as opposed to knowingly distributing porn to minors,) do not apply automatically, and require evaluation by a court. As of the writing of this page, such a thing has not occurred on the state level, and it is arguable that a theoretical case could be appealed and subsequently overruled by federal standards.
